Krings, the author of H is for HEMP, is widely recognized for her pioneering contributions to hemp sustainability and art. Her upcoming exhibition will showcase an experimental installation featuring Anthotypes—a form of photography that utilizes hemp-derived phytocolors to develop images on 100% hemp paper. Starting on October 9, Krings will embark on a three-day exposure process of these Anthotypes, with the final artwork revealed at the conference’s afterparty on October 12.

This exhibition will give visitors an opportunity to engage with hemp’s environmental promise, exploring how art and sustainability can converge in dynamic, thoughtful ways. The event is poised to be a creative highlight of the Hemp for the Future conference, which has already made successful stops in Italy and Morocco earlier this year.

This event is part of the Hemp for the Future conference organized by the Icelandic Hemp Association, HAMPUR FYRIR FRAMTÍDINA.

Salka has a nostalgic, glorifying outlook on drawing pictures associated with familiar, every-day images of engraved consumerism and laissez-faire utopias—at times linked with concepts of freedom and escapism. In the spirit of adding luster to mundane and decadent imagery, she attempts to capture a bitter-sweet feeling of disenchantment with a certain doomed promise of greatness.

Deriving from the situationist notion of the Spectacle, the artist plays with reshuffling the roles of the consumer & the objects of consumption. Her creative approach & process in realising the artworks is repetitive, stoical and meditative, based on a tactile craftsmanship and slow-production mode which come as a contrast to the capitalistic themes they depict.

In her practice, Salka deals with creating an atmosphere of relaxation at a speed of 120 km. She delegates on truth, repetition, the present and the future. She works across media, but with an emphasis on drawing and printmaking. Paradise II marks her first solo exhibition.

„The In-Between Land“ er þverfaglegt samstarfs- og sýningarverkefni, þar sem kafað er ofan í þjóðsögur, tímabil breytinga í lífi fólks og tengsl við náttúru. Listafólkið Dagmara Masłowska (PL/FI), Enda Ahlgren (FI) og Regn Sólmundur Evu (IS) vinna með miðla að eigin vali — málverk, vídeóverk, ljóð, dans, skúlptúr og innsetningu til að rannsaka mismunandi túlkanir á staðleysum eða millibilsástandi. Sýningin er afrakstur og úrvinnsla samtals og vinnu hópsins með viðfangsefnið.
"The In-Between Land" is an interdisciplinary collaboration and showcase which delves into the themes of folklore, life transitions and connection with nature. Artists Dagmara Masłowska (PL/FI), Enda Ahlgren (FI) and Regn Sólmundur Evu (IS) use their preferred mediums - painting, video, poetry, dance, sculpture and installation - to explore various expressions of liminal states. The exhibition is the result of the group's ongoing dialogue on this subject.

Dagmara Masłowska (b.1992 in Poznań, Poland) is a dancer, somatic guide and mother. She has been following teachers internationally, learning about the intersection of life and art. She performed with a Norwegian street theater called Stella Polaris, joined the DUENDE School for Ensemble Physical Theatre in Greece, Carte Blanche sensorial theater in Denmark and JINEN Butoh School by sensei Takenouchi Atsushi in Italy. Moving to Finland in 2020 marked a new chapter of slow-paced work with focus on sustainability and community life. After graduating from ISLO’s program for Dance and Somatics (Joensuu) in 2022 she started a project called Luomu Koreo / Organic Choreographies supported by KonstSamfundet. In her work she often combines contemplative and improvisational tools, being influenced by eco-somatics and in touch with despair while living in times of climate emergency.

Enda Ahlgren (b. 1992 in Naantali, Finland) is an artist and facilitator who operates in-between known categories and definitions. They often combine methods of performance art, portraiture and participatory art in their creative process. Enda holds a BA in Fine Arts from Kankaanpää Art School and is currently studying a MA programme in Contemporary Contexts of Arts at Turku Arts Academy. They have worked diversely in the field of art and culture – holding exhibitions, organizing events, and holding various roles in collaborative and community projects. A passion for understanding and creating change – both on a personal and societal level – connects everything in their practice.

Regn Sólmundur (b. 1998 in Reykjavik, Iceland) is a multimedia artist and poet. They graduated with a BA degree in fine arts from Iceland University of the Arts in 2023 and studied creative writing at MA level at the University of Iceland 2023-2024. Regn works with the theme of juxtaposing the sublime and the ordinary, consciousness and subconscious, spirituality and science and technology. Regn oftentimes lends humour and playfulness into their work, as these themes can seem dramatic and heavy. They have participated in over 20 exhibitions all around Iceland, and has hosted two solo exhibitions.

This exhibition is supported by the Embassy of Finland in Reykjavík and the Municipality of Reykjavik.

g(l)azed mirrors | Salóme Hollanders
g(l)azed mirrors er hönnunarlína eftir Salóme Hollanders sem samanstendur af þremur gerðum spegla; original g(l)azed mirror, mini g(L)azed mirror og tall g(L)azed mirror.

Grunnformin og geómetrísk abstraktlist hafa lengi heillað Salóme og er formlögun speglanna byggð á einu grunnformanna, hringnum. Með samsetningu hringformsins í ólíkum stærðum taka speglarnir á sig ósymmetríska og flæðandi lögun. Speglarnir eru lakkaðir með slitserku háglanslakki og eru fáanlegir í sjö mismunandi litum. Salóme hannar og framleiðir hvert eintak hér Íslandi í samstarfi við fagaðila og því fer mikil ást, vandvirkni og tími í gerð hvers spegils.

g(l)azed mirrors are a collection of mirrors designed by Salóme Hollanders that consists of three types; original g(l)azed mirror, mini g(l)azed mirror og tall g(l)azed mirror.

The fundamental geometric shapes as well as geometric abstract art have long fascinated Salóme. Therefore the figure of the mirrors is based on one of the fundamental geometric shapes, the circle. By combining circular shapes in different sizes, the mirrors take on asymmetrical and flowing form. The mirrors are varnished with a resistant high-gloss lacquer and are available in seven different colors. Salóme designs and manufactures each piece in Iceland in collaboration with skilled craftsmen, so a lot of love, care and time goes into the making of each mirror.

House of Error - Waning Moon | Sigríður Birna Matthiasdóttir
House of Error er glænýtt stafrænt tískuhús sem kemur til með að frumsýna sitt fyrsta “collection”, Dvínandi tungl. Dvínandi tungl heiðrar framlag kvenna til tækni og lista, og gagnrýnir í senn kerfislæga fordóma sem hafa jaðarsett konur í þessum geirum.Dvínandi tungl sækir innblástur í samband kvenna við hringrás tunglsins, framlag kvenna til vísinda, stafrænt handverk og dulspeki.

House of Error trúir að með aukinni valdeflingu kvenna munum við sem samfélag afnema kapítalisma og endur tengjast náttúrunni. Stafræn tíska er áhugaverður nýr miðill sem hefur fjölmarga framtíðarmöguleika.

Á sýningunni verður hægt að prófa stafrænar flíkur í viðbótarveruleika.

House of Error's debut digital fashion collection, "Waning Moon," stands at the intersection of technology, mysticism, and feminist empowerment. This collection transcends the conventional separation of myth and machine, embedding the narrative of female resilience and empowerment within its digital craftsmanship.

"Waning Moon" pays homage to women's historical and ongoing contributions to technology and the arts, critiquing systematic biases that have marginalized female voices in these fields. By integrating digital artistry with themes of ecological futures , non-linear storytelling, and myth-making, the collection advocates for a symbiotic relationship between technology and spirituality, challenging patriarchal narratives.

House of Error presents a vision of the future where technology serves as a medium for inclusive, equitable narratives, contributing to the discourse on gender, spirituality, and technology's role in society, in an abstract and playful way.

At the exhibition, attendees will be able to test digital garments in augmented reality.

Fró(u)n | Elín Margot and Antonía Berg
Fyrir Hönnunarmars, býður Fró(u)n gestum að tengjast náttúrunni radíkalt: með því að elska hana!

Antonía Berg og Elín Margot munu kynna Fró(u)n, safn jarðbundinna kynlifstækja gerða úr postúlíni og leir úr Berunesi í Berufirði, heimahögum Antoníu. Þær munu hýsa workshop í Reykjavík þar sem öllum er velkomið að koma og handgera sín eigin kynlífstæki úr postulíni og íslenskum jarðefnum. Hvernig tjáum við ást til jarðarinnar? Hvað myndi breytast ef við myndum hugsa um jörðina sem elskhuga, endurskoða umgengni okkar við hana og byggja upp nýtt samband sem byggir á ást fremur en ofnýtingu? Íslenskur jarðvegur er einstakur og á frekari athygli okkar skilið. Með því að nýta íslenskan leir og postulín við gerð kynlífsleikfanga beina Antonía Berg og Elín Margot ljósi að því hvernig hægt er að beita rótttækari leiðum til að endurnýta sjálfbæran jarðveg Íslands.

For Designmarch, Fró(u)n invites guests to connect with nature radically: by having sex with it!

Antonía Berg and Elín Margot will present Fró(u)n, a collection of earthy sex toys made with clay from Berunes fjord, and host a workshop where anyone can hand model their own dildo with porcelain.

The designers aim to create a fetish for the ground we live on by inviting users to treat their environment with love. The idea of the earth as a lover is to reconsider how we interact with the natural environment and create new relationships based on love rather than exploitation.

Icelandic ground is unique and needs more consideration; through the creation of sex toys made of Icelandic clay and porcelain, Antonía Berg and Elín Margot propose alternative ways to view and potentially interact with our environment.


Garg ritröð er vettvangur til að að rannsaka tungumál, raddir og rými. Garg er tilraunakennd útgáfa sem einblínir á fræðslu og samveru. Ritað og talað mál er hluti af félagslegri athöfn þar sem lesandinn og orðið eru í samtali. Garg vill taka orðið af síðunni og inn í rýmið. Í ritinu er ný íslensk þýðing á „AF SÍÐUNNI: Háværar kýr. Erindi og ljóð um að lesa upphátt“ eftir Ursula K. Le Guin. Þýðing eftir Helgu Dögg og Ölmu Mjöll. Ritstýrt og hannað af Helgu Dögg. Myndlýsing eftir Urtinu Hoxha.

Garg is a serial publication on the theme of language, voices and space. Garg is a practice in publishing, learning and togetherness. The written and spoken word is a part of a social act, the reader and writer are in dialogue, much like the reader and the audience. The publication is an attempt to extract words from the page and into the space.
The publication features a new translation into the Icelandic of Ursula K. Le Guin’s essay “OFF THE PAGE: Loud Cows. A Talk and a Poem About Reading Aloud.” Translated by Helga Dögg and Alma Mjöll. Edited and designed by Helga Dögg. Illustration by Urtina Hoxha.
